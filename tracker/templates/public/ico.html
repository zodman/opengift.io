GIFT Ecosystem

Software financing

Projects members receive donations in GIFTs according to their share of the project. The sum in the currency of the donation is transferred to the Exchange Fund.

 
Internal Exchanges

GIFT Exchanges set their orders to buy GIFTs from users. Users can sell GIFT, hold it or pay in it another users for their code or marketing service.

 
 
Free transactions

GIFTs transactions in the system are free. There is no commission.

Project Strategy

  • 2017

    The OpenGift Platform prototype launched. First projects registered on the Platform. Users fund development of projectsâ€™ new features. Investors in projects can receive a share of the amount donated through smart-contracts.

  • 2018

    Projects rating system and technological map implemented. It gets cheaper for companies to fund OSS developers directly through OpenGift avoiding the intermediaries. The projects successfully solving the most important problems become visible and easy to find for investors and funds.

  • 2019

    OpenGift goes fully opensource. A community of experts is created. Projects become interlinked in a tree to solve global technological problems. Donators can fund them online and observe in real time what exactly the funds are being spent on.

  • 2020

    GIFT-based economy is on the rise. OpenSource becomes way more popular than proprietary software. The community is rapidly developing creating millions of OS products designed to solve the specific problems of the donators. Developers get fairly paid for their contribution into OpenSource projects.
